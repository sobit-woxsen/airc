AI has permeated the world, be it with suggestions on what to watch on streaming sites to clinics and credit card fraud. Machine Learning (ML)—a technology that allows machines, instead of processing data, learns, evolves, and becomes better on its own is at the core of such intelligent systems. So what does it really mean by machine learning and how then does this learning cause the monumental change we see in the industries?

## The Problem: Translating Human-Like Learning to Machines

Even though significant strides have been made, real human-like learning in machines is a grand challenge. Whereas ML algorithms are very effective when dealing with large data sets, pattern recognition and prediction, classic models can frequently be hard to use when dealing with tasks that humans would feel are intuitive, such as reasoning with little data, cross-domain knowledge transfer, or discerning nuances. Common hurdles include:

**Data Dependency:** ML models are very demanding in terms of large size of labeled data unlike humans who can learn with few examples.

**Prejudice and Justice:** AI technologies have the potential to increase pre-existing prejudice, and critical issues of transparency, inclusivity, and ethical integrity emerge.

**Interpretability:** Due to the black-box characteristics of most ML models, their insights may be difficult to justify or even trust, particularly in such high stakes fields as healthcare and finance.

## The Solution: From Data to Actionable, Ethical Intelligence

The recent developments in the field of ML address these issues by:

**Few-shot and Transfer Learning:** Learning models to generalize more using less data or use other related data to learn.

**Explainable AI:** Creating AI that does not simply provide answers but also tells why those answers are the ones provided—these systems that make people feel and trust the choices being made.

**Responsible AI:** The AI developed comes with fairness and accountability in its spirit meaning that our solutions are ethical, transparent, and not subject to harmful bias.
